Female HealthWorker

A female nurse is a woman professionally trained and licensed to provide healthcare support, including patient care, administering medications, monitoring health, and offering emotional assistance. She often serves as a bridge between doctors and patients, ensuring proper communication and understanding.

Male HealthWorker

A male doctor is a man who has completed medical education and training to diagnose, treat, and prevent illnesses. Male doctors may specialize in various fields, such as surgery, internal medicine, or pediatrics, and are responsible for prescribing medication, performing procedures, and leading healthcare teams.

Female HealthWorker

A female doctor is a woman who has completed medical education and training to practice medicine. Like her male counterparts, she diagnoses and treats patients, prescribes medication, and may specialize in fields such as gynecology, cardiology, or general medicine, while balancing technical expertise with empathetic care.

Male HealthWorker

A male nurse is a man professionally trained and licensed to deliver nursing care. He performs similar duties to a female nurse, such as monitoring vitals, administering treatments, and assisting patients, while also contributing physical strength for tasks like patient transport or heavy lifting.
